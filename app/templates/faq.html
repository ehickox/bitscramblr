Frequently Asked Questions

How does bitscramblr work?

Bitscramblr is an application that will turn pseudoanonymous Bitcoin transactions into anonymous Bitcoin transactions. It is an extension of the concept of a shared wallet.

To ensure complete anonymity, bitscramblr blocks until adequately sized inputs are available from a source other than the origin.

Minimum transaction size?

We recommend a minimum transaction size of 0.01 BTC.

Maximum transaction size?

Currently, the maximum transaction size is 1 BTC. This will be raised in the future.

How long do transactions take?

To ensure complete anonymity, bitscramblr blocks until adequately sized inputs are available from a source other than the origin. If there is enough BTC in the system, transactions should happen as fast as a regular bitcoin transaction with standard fees (15-30 minutes).

What data is kept?

For quality assurance, data regarding the transaction origin is stored on the server temporarily. Once we verify that a transaction has gone through, all data corresponding to that transaction's origin is deleted. We anonymously store the number of transactions that have taken place for statistical purposes, and it makes for a nice way to prove our reliability.

Is bitscramblr open source?

Yes, under the Apache 2 License.

What are bitscramblr's limitations?

Bitscramblr is completely, mathematically, anonymous. This means that there is no direct path from your transaction's origin to its destination. So in that sense, its only limitation is that the transaction will not go through until there are adequately sized inputs available from another source.
